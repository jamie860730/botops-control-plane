## Design Context

### Users
BotOps Control Plane is used by product managers, bot operations specialists, support leads, knowledge owners, and compliance reviewers who need to manage customer-support automation across multiple channels. They are reviewing bot behavior under time pressure, comparing quality metrics, and deciding whether a bot version is safe to release.

### Brand Personality
Operational, trustworthy, and analytical. The interface should feel like a control room for support automation rather than a marketing dashboard or a chatbot demo.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dense, scan-friendly operational console with a dark navigation rail, cool neutral workspace, and `#4fc4cf` as the primary accent. Avoid Binance yellow/black, crypto-specific branding, oversized hero sections, decorative gradients, and playful SaaS landing-page patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Show the management loop, not only the bot conversation: intake, trace, knowledge, eval, error analysis, handoff, release, and audit.
2. Make risk and evidence visible: high-risk cases, citations, release gates, and handoff warnings should be easy to scan.
3. Keep pages compact and task-oriented: every screen should help a support admin make or verify a decision.
4. Use deterministic data and stable IDs in P0/P1 so demos, tests, and future backend contracts stay reliable.
5. Treat auditability as a product requirement: important admin actions should leave a visible trail.
